ITIS 3135 Website Evaluations

Space Jam 1996

Space Jam's website from 1996 is a nostalgic-looking website, showing the promotional material for the Space Jam Movie. The site has a mix of bright colors, animated GIFs, and an early internet web design.

  • Contrast: The website has bad contract, with bright text on a primarily light and dark background which makes it hard to read
  • Repitition: Most pages are the same, an image and some red text but sometimes the pages have different backgrounds and different text, some of the pages dont even work
  • Alignment: The alignment is good, the space between each other looks good
  • Proximity: The home elements are somewhat close to each other in a nice fashion so its not bad

    • The Space Jam website does not adhere well to the CRAP principles as well as it could have. Its design is very primitive and low effort, resulting in a visually chaotic and disappointing user experience. Also some of the links dont even work anymore.

Wall Street Journal

The Wall Street Journal's website is a professional platform providing news and analysis on global business, finance, and politics. The site features an organized layout, prioritizing readability and user experience.

  • Contrast: The website effectively uses contrast, having a base color scheme and font hierarchy.
  • Repitition: Repetition is pretty good, with consistent use of fonts, colors, and formatting across the site..
  • Alignment: The website follows a clear alignment strategy, with content organized in a grid layout
  • Proximity: Proximity is properly utilized with related elements placed close together, emphasizing their connection

The Wall Street Journal's website demonstrates a strong adherence to the CRAP principles. Its design has an effective use of contrast, repetition, alignment, and proximity, resulting in a visually pleasing and user-friendly experience.
